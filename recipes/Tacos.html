Vegetarian Tacos

Vegetarian Tacos are a vibrant and satisfying Mexican dish. They feature soft tortillas filled with a hearty mixture of black beans, corn, bell peppers, and onions, seasoned with cumin and paprika for a burst of flavor. Topped with creamy avocado slices and a dollop of sour cream or yogurt, and garnished with fresh cilantro, these tacos offer a delightful blend of textures and tastes. Served with a squeeze of lime, they provide a fresh, zesty finish. Perfect for a quick, flavorful meal!

Ingredients

  • 1 can of black beans (drained and rinsed)
  • 1 cup corn kernels (fresh or frozen)
  • 1 bell pepper (diced)
  • 1 small onion (diced)
  • 2-3 cloves garlic (minced)
  • 1 teaspoon cumin powder
  • 1 teaspoon paprika or chili powder
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 8 small tortillas (corn or flour)
  • 1 avocado (sliced)
  • 1/4 cup sour cream or yogurt (optional)
  • Fresh cilantro (for garnish)
  • Lime wedges (for serving)

Steps

  1. Prepare the Veggie Filling: Heat olive oil in a pan. Add the diced onions and garlic, saut√© until soft. Add the diced bell pepper, corn, and black beans. Season with cumin, paprika, salt, and pepper. Cook for 5-7 minutes until the vegetables are tender and flavors meld together.
  2. Warm the Tortillas: Heat the tortillas on a dry pan for 30 seconds on each side or until they are soft and warm.
  3. Assemble the Tacos: Spoon the veggie mixture onto each tortilla. Add avocado slices, a dollop of sour cream or yogurt, and garnish with fresh cilantro. Serve with lime wedges for a fresh, zesty touch.
