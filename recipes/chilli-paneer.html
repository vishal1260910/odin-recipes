Chilli Paneer

Chilli Paneer is a popular Indo-Chinese dish made by stir-frying cubes of paneer (Indian cottage cheese) with bell peppers, onions, and green chilies in a tangy, spicy sauce. The sauce is typically made from a blend of soy sauce, vinegar, chili sauce, and ketchup, giving it a balance of heat, sweetness, and tanginess. The paneer is often coated in a light batter and shallow fried before being tossed in the sauce, creating a crispy texture. It can be served as a snack, appetizer, or a main dish with fried rice or noodles.It's a perfect fusion of Indian flavors with a Chinese cooking style!

Ingredients

  • 200g paneer (cubed)
  • 1 onion (diced)
  • 1 green bell pepper (diced)
  • 2-3 green chilies (slit)
  • 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon vinegar
  • 2 tablespoons tomato ketchup
  • 1 tablespoon chili sauce (adjust to taste)
  • 1 tablespoon cornflour
  • 2 tablespoons water (for the slurry)
  • 1 teaspoon black pepper powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon sugar (optional)
  • Salt to taste
  • Oil (for frying and cooking)
  • Spring onions (for garnish)

Steps

  1. Fry the Paneer: Heat oil in a pan. Lightly fry the paneer cubes until golden brown, then remove and set aside.
  2. Prepare the Sauce: In the same pan, leave a bit of oil and saut√© the diced onions, bell peppers, and green chilies on high heat for 2-3 minutes, until they slightly soften but remain crunchy. Add the ginger-garlic paste and stir for 30 seconds
  3. Add Sauces: Lower the heat slightly, then add soy sauce, vinegar, tomato ketchup, and chili sauce. Mix well.
  4. Thicken the Sauce: Dissolve the cornflour in 2 tablespoons of water to make a slurry, then add it to the pan. Stir continuously until the sauce thickens.
  5. Toss the Paneer: Add the fried paneer to the sauce and toss everything together, making sure the paneer is well-coated.
  6. Season: Add salt, black pepper, and sugar if desired. Mix everything thoroughly. Garnish and Serve:
  7. Garnish with chopped spring onions and serve hot with fried rice or noodles.
